ALC (Auto Light Control)

The Auto Light Control Task (ALCTask) dynamically adjusts the PWM signals sent to the left and right side lights based on real-time readings from LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) sensors. Filtered sensor data is read securely using a mutex to ensure thread-safe access across the system. Each LDR value is processed through a calibration function (ProcessLDRValue) to normalize and enhance its accuracy. The resulting brightness levels are applied via hardware PWM channels to maintain balanced lighting. This allows the vehicle to automatically adapt to different lighting conditions, enhancing safety and energy efficiency. The task runs periodically and updates light levels every 200ms, ensuring a responsive behavior in changing environments. It operates independently from driver input, enabling full automation of side lighting. This task contributes to a smarter vehicle system by reducing reliance on manual lighting control.
